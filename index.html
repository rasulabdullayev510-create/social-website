Overview

Understanding Italian colonialism and its lasting impact on Libya

About This Site

From 1911 to 1943, Italy occupied and colonized the territory now known as Libya. What began as a calculated military invasion framed as a "civilizing mission" left deep marks on Libyan society, culture, land, and identity — marks that continue to shape the country today.

This site explores Italian imperialism in Libya across four key topics, followed by a concise summary. Each page is written to be informative and easy to follow. Use the navigation above to move between pages.

"Libya was not an empty desert waiting to be 'civilized.' It was home to diverse peoples with their own languages, religions, economies, and traditions — all of which were dramatically disrupted by Italian colonial rule."
